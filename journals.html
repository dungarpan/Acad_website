Letter from the Editor

The International Journal of Computers and Their Applications is published four times a year (March, June, September and December), with the purpose of providing a forum for state-ofthe- art research and development in the theory and design of computers as well as current innovative activities in the applications of computers. In contrast to other journals, this journal encourages contributions in real world applications of emerging computer technology, as well as advances in theoretical areas. Current topics of interest include, but are not limited to: cyber security, networks, intelligent systems, parallel and distributed computing, software and information engineering, and computer applications (for example, medicine, business, and education).

The encouragement of applied contributions implies that papers dealing with innovative applications of computing technology, or ones that may clarify how new theoretical results may be effectively used in real problems, are of interest. Theoretical papers are also welcome and are expected to include examples and case studies to illustrate the applicability of the theory. All papers are subject to a thorough peer review. The key criterion in the review process, as in other journals, is whether the paper contains a novel contribution to the computing area which the Editorial Board feels is sufficient to be of significant interest to the current/future computing research and application domains.

The editorial board includes a group of Associate Editors. The Associate Editors bring a substantial background and experience to the reviewing and journal development processes. An important goal of the editorial board is to minimize the time delay between paper submission and publication, without compromising the reviewing process. This is promoted through carefully defined reviewing procedures. We look forward to the challenge of maintaining this strategy as the journal activities grow.

Further information on the requirements/directions for paper submission, as well as information on past journal papers, and ordering journal copies and subscriptions is provided through the links to other pages.

Gordon K. Lee
Editor-In-Chief
